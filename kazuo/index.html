1954-1968 • First Style
 The atypical projects make use of Japanese architecture to expose the form and syntax of Japanese architecture—its modality—then utilise it to expose the idea of Japanese anti-space. The group act like machines to extract Japanese structures—abstract and tectonic—their physical forms effectuating the operation and acting as medium to communicate the output. The buildings operate in and not in the Japanese tradition—exactly they use Japanese construction in order to explicitly reject the object and to promote form.

Extra

1961 • Umbrella House The Umbrella House activates the negation of pretext—making the object into an example. This negation of exteriority—of division from itself—makes the house original. Being original the object gets attached to the identity Shinohara and the identity Shinohara gets attached to the object. Like this the Umbrella House starts the war between Shinohara and the object—between identity and form.

Emptiness

1964 • The Japanese Conception of Space The Werner Blaser 1955 publication Tempel und Teehaus made the tatami mat plan of Katsura Imperial Palace into the facsimile of the Miesian grid. The photography and the graphic design of the publication acting in synchrony to equivocate the two geometries. The Shinohara text The Japanese Conception Space published in 1964 in ja 39 negates any possibility to equivocate the Japanese timber frame to the modernist space frame. In the text Shinohara sets out the opposition—the unreal can not be like the real—to identically oppose the logic of emptiness to the medium of the grid. Basically Japanese architecture can not be imaged—imitated—letting it escape language.

1968–74 • Second Style
 The five projects included in the second style all produce a big space that is in the mode [not style] of the shinden-shoin style. The extra space—stripped of symbolism—negates the image of Japanese tradition and by default can not imitate the Japanese shoin—exactly it reproduces it. Shinohara constructs performativity—the dialectical mode—in concrete form. Collectively the projects demonstrate the gap—interspace—between method and object ergo between idea and logos. Exactly the form is the function and the model of interspace. Like this the houses all work to show that system is type.

1972–4 • Tanikawa House Radical simplification is made into exactly what it is. The practical function is non-existent so that the pretext—the negation of substitution—is exposed. The physical division of space now has no visible form—identically to the poetry of Shuntaro Tanikawa the object is hyper–literal. The antinomical nomenclature of the space machine [space being non-existent in Japan] is identically a machine that produces material emptiness.

1977 • The Third Style
 The text, in three parts, documents the trip Shinohara made to West Africa in 1976 and starts the use of the savage nomenclature he lifts from Claude Levi–Strauss. In it Shinohara goes back to the First, Second, and Third Style logics and gets to the reality that he is is still in 'all–out war' between 'abstraction and concreteness.'
